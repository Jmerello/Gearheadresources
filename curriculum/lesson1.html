Lesson 1: Safety Protocols

Lesson Overview

Safety is the foundation of any successful apprenticeship. This lesson covers essential shop safety protocols that every technician must follow to ensure a safe working environment.

Basic Shop Safety

  • Slips, Trips, and Falls: Keep floors clean and dry. Mark wet areas clearly and ensure tools and materials are stored safely.
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Always wear safety glasses, closed-toed shoes, gloves, and hearing protection when necessary.
  • Proper Attire: Avoid loose clothing or jewelry that could get caught in machinery. Always tie back long hair.

Basic Electrical Safety

  • Electrical Awareness: Disconnect power sources before working on electrical components.
  • Hand Placement: Use insulated tools and avoid touching live circuits with bare hands.

Work Area Tidiness

  • Maintain a clean and organized workspace to prevent hazards.
  • Dispose of waste materials properly and keep walkways clear.
  • No food or open drinks in the work area to avoid contamination and accidents.

Vehicle Lift Safety

  • Ensure vehicles are properly secured on lifts before working underneath.
  • No unauthorized personnel, including customers and visitors, are allowed under lifted vehicles.
  • Regularly inspect lifts for wear and proper function.

Practice Exercises

Complete the following tasks to reinforce your understanding of safety protocols:

  1. Identify three potential hazards in your current workspace and explain how to mitigate them.
  2. List the PPE required for different shop tasks and explain their importance.
  3. Demonstrate the correct procedure for securing a vehicle on a lift.
