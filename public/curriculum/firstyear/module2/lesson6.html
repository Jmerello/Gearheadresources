Lesson 6: Suspension System Maintenance

Lesson Overview

The suspension system is vital for handling and ride comfort. In this lesson, we'll explore how to inspect, maintain, and repair the suspension components.

Suspension System Overview

  • Components: Shocks, struts, springs, control arms, ball joints, and bushings.
  • Function: The suspension system absorbs road bumps, supports the vehicle's weight, and maintains tire contact with the road for better handling.

Shock Absorber Inspection and Replacement

  • Tools Needed: Jack, jack stands, wrench, shock absorbers, spring compressors (if needed).
  • Steps: Lift the vehicle and remove the wheel. Inspect the shock absorber for signs of leakage or wear. Replace if necessary, following the vehicle's manufacturer recommendations.
  • Safety: Use proper lifting techniques and secure the vehicle before working. Be cautious when handling compressed springs.

Ball Joint and Control Arm Inspection

  • Inspect for Wear: Check ball joints for play and control arms for any signs of bending or damage.
  • Replacement: If the ball joints are worn, replace them immediately as they are critical for vehicle control and alignment.

Spring and Bushing Maintenance

  • Inspect Springs: Check the suspension springs for cracks, corrosion, or sagging. If necessary, replace with appropriate springs.
  • Inspect Bushings: Examine bushings for wear or deterioration. Worn bushings can cause handling issues and should be replaced promptly.

Practice Exercises

Complete the following tasks to reinforce your understanding of suspension system maintenance:

  1. Describe the role of shock absorbers and the steps for inspecting them for wear or leakage.
  2. Explain how to check for wear in ball joints and control arms. What are the consequences of failing to replace them?
  3. Identify the signs of wear in suspension springs and bushings. How can this affect vehicle handling?
