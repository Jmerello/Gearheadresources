Lesson 4: Oil and Fluid Maintenance

Lesson Overview

In this lesson, we will explore the essential task of maintaining a vehicle's oils and fluids. Proper fluid management is crucial for engine performance and longevity.

Engine Oil Change

  • Tools Needed: Wrench, oil filter wrench, oil pan, funnel, new oil, and filter.
  • Steps: Lift the vehicle, drain the old oil, replace the oil filter, and refill with fresh oil. Ensure you use the correct oil grade for the vehicle's specifications.
  • Safety: Wear gloves to prevent skin contact with hot oil. Ensure the vehicle is securely lifted to avoid accidents.

Transmission Fluid Maintenance

  • Tools Needed: Wrench, funnel, transmission fluid, gloves.
  • Steps: Locate the transmission dipstick, check the fluid level, and add the recommended transmission fluid if necessary. Make sure to follow the manufacturer's specifications for fluid type and quantity.
  • Safety: Ensure the engine is off before opening the fluid reservoir. Be cautious with hot components around the transmission.

Brake Fluid Maintenance

  • Tools Needed: Wrench, brake fluid, clean container, gloves.
  • Steps: Locate the brake fluid reservoir, remove the cap, and check fluid levels. If necessary, top up with the correct brake fluid type.
  • Safety: Be cautious not to spill brake fluid, as it can damage vehicle paint. Always dispose of old brake fluid properly.

Engine Coolant Maintenance

  • Tools Needed: Coolant, funnel, gloves.
  • Steps: Check the coolant level in the radiator and coolant reservoir. Refill as needed with the appropriate coolant mixture (usually a 50/50 ratio of coolant to water).
  • Safety: Never open the radiator cap when the engine is hot. Always allow the vehicle to cool before checking or adding coolant.

Practice Exercises

Complete the following tasks to reinforce your understanding of oil and fluid maintenance:

  1. List the tools needed to perform an oil change and describe the steps involved.
  2. Explain how to check and top up transmission fluid. Why is it important to use the correct fluid?
  3. Identify the steps for maintaining brake fluid and coolant fluid, and explain why each is important for vehicle safety and performance.
