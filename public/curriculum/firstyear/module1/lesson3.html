Lesson 3: Basic Vehicle Maintenance

Lesson Overview

Basic vehicle maintenance is a key skill for technicians. In this lesson, we will cover the fundamental maintenance tasks every technician should know.

Engine Oil Change

  • Tools Needed: Wrench, oil filter wrench, oil pan, funnel, new oil, and filter.
  • Steps: Lift the vehicle, drain the old oil, replace the filter, and fill with fresh oil.
  • Safety: Wear gloves and be mindful of hot engine components. Dispose of the used oil properly.

Tire Maintenance

  • Check Tire Pressure: Ensure tires are inflated to the recommended pressure to avoid premature wear or accidents.
  • Tire Rotation: Rotate tires every 6,000 to 8,000 miles to promote even wear.
  • Inspect Tire Condition: Look for signs of wear, such as bald spots or punctures, and replace tires as needed.

Fluid Checks

  • Coolant: Check coolant levels regularly and ensure the engine doesn't overheat.
  • Brake Fluid: Ensure brake fluid is at the proper level to maintain braking efficiency.
  • Transmission Fluid: Check transmission fluid regularly to prevent issues with shifting and transmission performance.

Practice Exercises

Complete the following tasks to reinforce your understanding of basic vehicle maintenance:

  1. List the steps for changing engine oil and explain the tools needed for the task.
  2. Describe the importance of tire rotation and how often it should be done.
  3. Identify three key fluids in a vehicle and explain how to check their levels.
